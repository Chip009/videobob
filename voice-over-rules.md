# TikTok Language Guidelines (English)

## Script shape (exact)
- 6 lines total (one per 4s scene)
- Single-topic story only (no mixed fact list)
- Line 1: hook with immediate curiosity
- Lines 2-5: escalating explanation with concrete verbs
- Line 6: open question that invites comments

## Narrative map for 6 lines
1. Hook
2. Setup/Escalation
3. Mechanism/Twist
4. Stakes
5. Validation/Result
6. Cliffhanger question

## Style rules
- Write like spoken TikTok voice-over, not academic prose.
- Keep lines SHORT and PUNCHY (must fit 4 seconds when spoken).
- Prefer plain words over technical jargon.
- Use tension and payoff sequence.
- Every line should make the next line feel necessary.

## Word-count guardrails (critical)
- Default line length: **7-10 words**.
- Sweet spot for OpenAI TTS at speed 1.0: **8-9 words**.
- Hard ceiling without explicit approval: **11 words**.
- If any generated VO line exceeds timing budget, rewrite that line to **7-9 words** and regenerate all 6 VO clips.

## Hook rules
- Lead with a contradiction, hidden mechanism, or shocking scale.
- Avoid weak openers ("Today we'll discuss...").
- No fluff greetings.

## Clarity rules
- One idea per line.
- If a line needs extra commas to make sense, rewrite it.
- Use concrete images: "frozen tusk", "bright sunlight", "lab embryo".

## Retention rules
- Place strongest reveal around lines 3-4.
- Keep unresolved tension for line 6 question.
- Do not close the topic too neatly before the final line.

## Tone
- Confident, sharp, curiosity-first.
- High energy without cringe.
- No cringe slang spam.
