# Hook Patterns for Science Short-Form Video

Use these as pattern banks, not copy-paste lines. Adapt to your topic.

## High-performing patterns
- "This sounds fake but it's true..."
- "If this doesn't shock you, slide 3 will."
- "You do this daily, but science says it's wild."
- "Nobody explains this clearly, so here it is."
- "Wait until you hear what happens next..."

## Candidate hook bank (curated)
- Swipe. These 5 facts sound fake but are true.
- If you stop at scene 1, you'll miss the craziest part.
- Your brain will hate what scene 4 reveals.
- You use this every day and it's weirder than you think.

## Hook scoring rubric
Score each candidate 1-10 on:
- Stop power (does it interrupt the scroll?)
- Novelty (is this something the viewer hasn't seen 100 times?)
- Clarity (can it be understood in 2 seconds?)
- Curiosity gap (does it demand that the next scene be watched?)
- Niche fit (does it match the channel's tone and audience?)

Composite score = average of the five dimensions.
Recommend the top 2 scoring candidates to the user.

## What to avoid
- Generic openings ("Today we talk about...")
- Reused hook wording within 7 days
- Weak line 1 that does not force scene 2 retention
- Clickbait that the video doesn't deliver on
