# Science Facts for VideoBob

## Mind-bending & Bizarre Science Facts

- **Radioactive Snacks:** Bananas contain Potassium-40, making them slightly radioactive. You'd need to eat about 10 million bananas at once to die of radiation poisoning, but you'd definitely die of "banana overdose" first.
- **Cosmic Weight:** A single teaspoon of a neutron star would weigh about 6 billion tons. That's roughly the weight of the entire human population... if everyone was made of lead and stacked in one spot.
- **DNA Highway:** If you uncoiled all the DNA in your body and stretched it out end-to-end, it would reach from Earth to Pluto and back—17 times.
- **Stomach vs. Steel:** Your stomach acid (pH 1.0 to 2.0) is strong enough to dissolve stainless steel. It doesn't dissolve you only because your stomach lining replaces itself every few days.
- **Internal Brewery:** There's a rare condition called "Auto-brewery syndrome" where your gut bacteria turn sugary and starchy foods into actual alcohol, essentially getting you drunk just by eating a bagel.
- **Hidden Underwater Waterfall:** The Denmark Strait cataract between Greenland and Iceland drops 11,500 feet underwater—three times the height of Angel Falls—and moves more water than Niagara Falls, driven by cold dense water sinking.
- **Uranus' True Color:** It's not just generic blue; it is a distinct pale blue-green because methane in its upper atmosphere absorbs red light from the sun, reflecting the rest back into space.
- **One Global Ocean:** There aren't really five oceans; 71% of Earth's surface is covered by one single, connected body of water that circulates around the globe.
- **Earthquakes are Constant:** There are about 20,000 earthquakes detected worldwide every year—that’s roughly 55 every single day, though most are too weak to feel.
- **The Moon Fits the Planets:** The average distance to the Moon is 384,400 km. You could fit every other planet in the solar system (Mercury through Neptune) in that gap with space to spare.
- **Earth's Rotation Cheat:** Earth doesn't take 24 hours to rotate once on its axis; it takes 23 hours, 56 minutes, and 4 seconds. The extra ~4 minutes is why we have leap years (sort of).
- **Spring Tides:** When the Sun and Moon align (New or Full Moon), their gravitational pulls combine to create "spring tides"—the highest high tides and lowest low tides, named for "springing forth," not the season.
- **Biggest Quake Ever:** The 1960 Valdivia earthquake in Chile was a magnitude 9.5. It was so powerful it sent tsunami waves across the Pacific that hit Hawaii, Japan, and the Philippines.
- **Neptune's Long Year:** Neptune takes 165 Earth years to orbit the Sun. Since its discovery in 1846, it has only completed one full orbit (in 2011).
- **Jupiter's Giant Storm:** The Great Red Spot on Jupiter is a storm that has been raging for at least 300 years and is wide enough to fit the entire Earth inside it.
- **Sunlight Delay:** It takes sunlight about 8 minutes and 20 seconds to reach Earth. If the Sun disappeared right now, we wouldn't know for over 8 minutes.
- **Volcano Count:** There are about 1,500 potentially active volcanoes worldwide, not counting the continuous belt of volcanoes on the ocean floor.
- **The Pressure Rule:** For every 10 meters (33 feet) you go down in the ocean, the pressure increases by one atmosphere (approx 14.7 psi). At the bottom of the Mariana Trench, it's like an elephant standing on your thumb.
- **Floating Saturn:** Saturn is the only planet in our solar system that is less dense than water. If you had a bathtub big enough, Saturn would float.
- **The Ghostly Treetop Glow (2024 Discovery):** During thunderstorms, tree leaves can emit faint blue electrical discharges called "coronae." Scientists captured this on camera for the first time in 2024; it's invisible to the human eye but paints entire canopies in UV light.
- **The Gaming Bladder Hack:** "Inhibitory spillover" is a psychological phenomenon where physical self-control (like holding your bladder) leaks into mental tasks. Research shows people with full bladders make less impulsive decisions and can perform better in high-stakes video games.
- **The Smell of Space:** Astronauts consistently report that outer space has a distinct odor, often described as burnt steak, hot metal, or welding fumes. NASA actually commissioned a perfume maker to recreate this "space-stink" for training purposes.
- **The Atomic Priesthood:** To protect humans 10,000 years in the future from nuclear waste, researchers proposed an "Atomic Priesthood"—a group that uses myths, rituals, and legends to warn people away from danger zones long after modern languages are forgotten.
- **Thunderstorm Leaf Defense:** The blue "coronae" on leaf tips aren't just for show; they can actually singe the tips of the leaves. This suggests that repeated lightning-adjacent discharges may have shaped the evolution of tree canopies to minimize electrical damage.
